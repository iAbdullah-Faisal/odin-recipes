Description:

Kabsa is a fragrant Saudi Arabian rice dish often served during special occasions. It‚Äôs made by cooking rice with spiced meat (usually chicken, lamb, or beef) in a rich tomato-based broth infused with aromatic spices like cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon. The dish is both hearty and flavorful, offering a perfect blend of savory and slightly sweet notes.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 lb chicken pieces (or lamb/beef)
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, grated
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground cloves
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the chicken pieces to the pot and brown them on all sides.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and grated carrots. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the tomatoes soften.
  6. Add the spices: cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, black pepper, turmeric, and salt. Stir well to coat the chicken and vegetables with the spices.
  7. Add the chicken broth to the pot and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.
  8. Add the drained rice to the pot, stirring gently to combine. Ensure the rice is submerged in the broth. If needed, add more broth or water.
  9. Cover the pot and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes until the rice is tender and has absorbed all the liquid.
